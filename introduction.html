Introduction

Guitar?

The guitar is a fretted musical instrument that typically has six strings. It is usually held flat against the player's body and played by strumming or plucking the strings with the dominant hand, while simultaneously pressing selected strings against frets with the fingers of the opposite hand. A plectrum or individual finger picks may also be used to strike the strings. The sound of the guitar is projected either acoustically, by means of a resonant chamber on the instrument, or amplified by an electronic pickup and an amplifier.

Parts of Guitar-


1.Body - The main part of the guitar. The body is large and hollow on an acoustic to amplify the sound. It can be solid and smaller on an electric guitar.


2.Neck - The neck sticks out from the body and connects to the headstock. The neck holds the frets and the fingerboard.


3.Headstock - The top of the guitar where the tuning pegs sit. Connects to the end of the neck.


4.Strings - The standard guitar has six strings. They are typically steel for electric and acoustic. They are nylon for classical guitars.


5.Frets - Hard metal strips that are installed into the fingerboard on top of the neck. The frets provide a place for the string to end when pressing down with the finger. Each fret and string represent a musical note.


6.Pegs/tuners - The pegs, or tuners, sit in the headstock and hold one end of a string. By turning the pegs, the tightness of the string can be adjusted and the guitar can be tuned.


7.Nut - The nut sits at the end of the neck. It provides an ending point for the vibration of the string so open notes can be played.


8.Fingerboard - The fingerboard is on top of the neck. The frets are installed into the fingerboard. This is where the strings are pressed down to create notes.


9.Bridge - The bridge sits on the sound board and is where the other end of the strings are attached. The bridge helps to translate the vibration from the strings down to the soundboard.


10.Pickguard - Helps to protect the soundboard from getting scratched when playing.
